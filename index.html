Solar Dominion is a spaceship skirmish wargame and science-fiction universe

Set in the 2200s as humanity has expanded into the stars, Solar Dominion follows the conflicts between the major human factions as they vie for space and resources as well as their existential fight against the crystaline alien terror of the Outsider.

Human Factions

  • United Nations of Mankind

    Founded from the power structures of old Earth, the UNM control roughly half of the settled planets in the galaxy. Favouring technological sophistication over numbers, the UNM Navy is a highly elite force to be reckoned with.

  • Independent Planet Protection Pact

    Hundreds of independent planets, roughly half of all settled worlds, form the IP3. Highly individualistic, the Pact navy favours simple, easy to maintain technology and overwhelming numbers.

  • Void Born Union

    Forgoing settling planets, the VBU build massive space-based habitats and travel through the ancient natural wormhole networks. Their anarchistic tendencies make them easy friends for pirates and smugglers.

The Tabletop Game

Fast paced, with simple mechanics that lend themselves to tactical thinking, Solar Dominion can be taught to new players in about ten minutes. With a realistic newtonian movement system, it is one of the few games on the market that correctly simulates the behaviour of objects in space whilst not bogging players down in mathematics or book keeping.

Combat in Solar Dominion is quick, simple and decisive. Ships lose systems when damaged, giving real impact and consequences to combat and the system of cards for each ship removes any fiddly record keeping required in tracking damage other similar games.

Numerous scenarios are available in the rulebook from simple fleet battles, VIP extraction to ambushes. Quick and simple victory point allocation prevents disputes about victory and drives players to interesting, thematic actions. Expansions include rules for incorporating civilian ships and stations into play for added intrigue.
